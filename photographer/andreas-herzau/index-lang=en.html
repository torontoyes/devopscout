For over 20 years now Andras Herzau has been working as a freelance photographer. He has carried out assignments all over the world with focus on photojournalism and photographic essays, working on free assignments and projects as well as on behalf of German and international magazines and newspapers. He is also active as a visiting lecturer and guest professor at institutions such as the University of Applied Sciences Bielefeld and the State University of Design, Media and Arts in Karlsruhe.

Herzau is one of those committed photographers who has extended the boundaries of classical photojournalism in independent and often startling ways. One of the issues concerning him is to examine familiar image stereotypes, which he attempts to question by means of his own intensive images. In the process, he uses his visual language to combine essayistic-narrative and analytical-abstract elements to create intense visual stories which he prefers to publish as book projects.
